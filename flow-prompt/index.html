What the prompt words actually mean

    
So you can tweak it with confidence instead of guessing.

    

      
12 FPS stop-motion

      
Plays at 12 frames a second instead of a smooth 24 to 30, so the motion looks choppy and handmade, like real stop-motion. This is what gives it that crafty, non-AI feel. Take it out and it goes smooth and generic.

      
Editorial paper collage

      
The whole look is built from torn paper, tape, and magazine cutouts, like a scrapbook or a bold magazine spread. It's the core aesthetic.

      
Halftone

      
The tiny dot pattern from old newspaper and comic-book printing. Adds a printed, retro texture instead of a clean digital look.

      
Kinetic typography

      
Text that animates. Words fly in, stamp down, and land one at a time in sync with your voice. It's what makes each line hit.

      
Torn-paper wipe

      
A transition where a paper rip reveals the next shot, instead of a plain cut or fade.

      
Crash zoom & whip pan

      
Fast camera moves. A crash zoom punches in quickly, a whip pan snaps sideways. They keep the energy high so it doesn't feel static.

      
Electric yellow accent

      
One bright signature color used for emphasis. Keeping a single accent makes the video feel bold and consistent. Swap it for your own brand color if you like.
